Weizi Li / 李玮孜

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at University of California, Riverside. I founded and direct the Fluidic City Lab.

Before the faculty life, I was a Postdoctoral Hammer Fellow at MIT and I received my Ph.D. from UNC Chapel Hill. I have also spent time at Disney Research, Lenovo Research, National University of Singapore, and George Mason University.

I am passionate about leveraging multi-agent simulation and machine learning to advance cyber-physical infrastructure for urban environments. The main themes of my research are autonomous systems and urban mobility.

My projects are generously supported by National Science Foundation, U.S. Department of Transportation, U.S. Department of Energy, and NVIDIA.
